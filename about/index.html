About this blog

To make my writing more structured and predictable, every post on Popov R&D Blog belongs to one of the following categories:

  • “break-down”: [5-7 min read] working horse of my knowledge accumulative approach. I take a complex concept, system or frameworks to dissect and analyse how it works internally and why, explore applicability and scope;
  • “opinion”: [2-3 min read] oftentimes, I just want to express an opinion on some disputable topic, fixate on it and provide my arguments to back it.
  • “overview”: [4-5 min read] first / fresh personal view on some new approach,system, concept, pattern, framework or programming language.
  • “deep-dive”: [10-15 min read] I plunge deeper than “break-down”, exploring code, providing schemes and detailed analysis;
  • “benchmark”: [3-4 min read] I do a real experiment and publish its results with numbers, conditions, and test configurations and try to interpret its results.
  • “analysis”: [3-5 min read] I research what others say on a given topic or idea, provide my own thoughts and make a conclusion.
My style

You won’t find “how-to” tutorials here. Frameworks, APIs, and tools evolve too quickly — step-by-step setups often become obsolete within months.

Instead, I focus on principles, mechanisms, and reasoning that stay valid across versions and technologies.

My goal is not to show which button to click, but to explain why the system behaves as it does — so you can apply that understanding anywhere.
